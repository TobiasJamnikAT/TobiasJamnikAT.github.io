Warfare


In Crusader Kings 3, there are various methods of acquiring land. While it is possible to strategically marry into an inheritance line and subsequently resort to murder to obtain property, the simpler approach is to conquer domains.


To achieve this, it is crucial to have strong allies and lead a sizeable army. Each type of terrain requires troops with specific strengths; light infantry and archers are preferable for fighting in mountains, whereas horses can be a liability for your army.


Conducting warfare is not overly complex. When launching an attack, you must send your troops and those of your allies to the enemy, whereas when defending, you must safeguard your land. The most efficient way to secure victory is to weaken the enemy forces and then attack valuable cities. Capturing important figures can aid in winning the war.


After successfully capturing enemy domains, it is imperative to establish dominance and control. Otherwise, the inhabitants may start rebellions and contribute less taxes.
