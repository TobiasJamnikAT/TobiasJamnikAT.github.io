Culture

Crusader Kings 3 features a variety of different cultures, each with their own unique traits and characteristics. Some of the cultures that players can encounter in the game include Norse, English, Scottish, Irish, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Byzantine, Arabic, Persian, Indian, and many more.


Each of these cultures has its own set of cultural innovations, which are essentially unique technologies that can be unlocked as the culture advances. For example, Norse cultures can unlock innovations like raiding and berserkers, while Indian cultures can unlock innovations like caste and dharma.


Cultures in Crusader Kings 3 also have unique traditions and customs. For example, some cultures place a strong emphasis on family and dynastic ties, while others prioritize religious devotion or martial prowess. These traditions and customs can impact gameplay, as they can affect things like political alliances, military strength, and cultural identity.


Overall, the cultural diversity in Crusader Kings 3 adds depth and complexity to the game, as players must navigate the unique challenges and opportunities presented by each culture they encounter.



Faith

Depending on where you start in the world, there are different beliefs and traditions. The game features two major categories of faiths: Abrahamic and Pagan.


The choice of faith can have a significant impact on a player's strategy and the actions of their characters. An Islamic ruler may have access to unique buildings, or justifications for war, against non-believers. Similarly, a Catholic ruler may have the ability to excommunicate their enemies, which can significantly weaken their position.


Pagan rulers, on the other hand, have a different set of challenges and opportunities. They may need to engage in raiding and conquest to gain power and resources, and they can also make use of various pagan religious mechanics such as sacrifice and vassalization of religious leaders.


Overall, the choice of faith in Crusader Kings 3 can shape the course of the game and the fate of a player's dynasty. It can affect relationships with other characters, influence the types of alliances and marriages that are possible, and determine which religious buildings can be constructed.
